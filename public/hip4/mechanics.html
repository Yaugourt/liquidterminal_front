Contest lifecycle — V1 (0x4fd7…44bd)
  • 1
    OWNER calls createContest(contestId, entryFee) — creates the market and sets the exact entry fee in wei.
  • 2
    Anyone deposits exactly entryFee HYPE — deposit(contestId, sideId) payable; emits DepositReceived. 1 deposit max per address per side.
  • 3
    Deposit deadline enforced on-chain — after it, new deposits revert ("Deposit deadline passed").
  • 4
    OWNER calls publishMerkleRoot(contestId, merkleRoot, rewardPool) once the contest is finalized — ties the published reward pool to the root.
  • 5
    Winners call claim(contestId, sideId, bytes32[] proof) (selector historically elusive on V1; shape per reconstructed ABI). Platform skim is reflected via platformFeeBps() / reward pool accounting on the legacy deployment.
  • 6
    After claims: OWNER may call sweepUnclaimed(contestId) for residual flows (see revert strings).
  • !
    Refund path: refund(contestId, sideId, user) before the Merkle root is locked in.
Contest lifecycle — V2 (0x6d86…cf07)
  • 1
    Same createContest / finalizeContest / publishMerkleRoot(contestId, root, rewardPool) owner flow as V1, plus Pausable and Ownable2Step.
  • 2
    deposit(contestId, sideId, deadline) payable — user-supplied deadline must satisfy block.timestamp <= deadline; emits Deposit (new topic0 vs V1 DepositReceived).
  • 3
    Off-chain builder constructs a Merkle tree whose leaves are keccak256(abi.encodePacked(index, recipient, amount)) — index is arbitrary but must be unique per payout slot; contract tracks claims in a per-contest bitmap keyed by index.
  • 4
    Winners call claim(contestId, index, recipient, amount, merkleProof[]). Payout pushes amount wei to recipient; reverts with "Invalid proof" / "Already claimed" as appropriate.
  • 5
    Platform fee is not a fixed platformFeeBps() view — it is the spread between totalPool, contestRewardPools(contestId), and amounts already paid out. Owner withdraws via withdrawPlatformFee(contestId, amount) (emits FeesWithdrawn).
  • 6
    Refund path: refund / refundTo(..., recipient) for directed payouts.
