REJECTED - placements that were considered and should not ship
× Header centre (Header.tsx, the widest empty space in the shell) - persistent, above the fold, next to the search. This is the single most valuable pixel on the site and selling it is what turns a terminal into a billboard. Etherscan does it: 4 ad slots on its home, one mid-content between the chart and latest blocks. × Sidebar footer band - persistent on every route, survives the 56px collapse. Same objection, plus it competes with the Mainnet status dot, which is functional. × Onboarding step 8 "our sponsors" - first contact with a new user, spent on a logo. × Chart co-watermark (ChartWatermark.tsx) - screenshots travel; a sponsor mark riding LT's data into Twitter is exactly the "je gagne quand tu trades" posture STRATEGY_ANNEXES.md:428 warns damages the grant pitch. × An uncapped cheap logo tier - Vue.js sells 36 Gold seats at $500/mo with no cap; a visible share is now Instagram-follower farms and betting sites. A cheap unbounded tier becomes an ad network on its own.

Every credible data platform observed shows zero patron logos on its data screens. The ones that monetise the product surface (Blockscout, Etherscan, DexScreener) are also the ones whose neutrality gets questioned.

growthepie - a public good funded the same way LT wants to be - independently arrived at the exact split this study proposes: 4 data-source logos on /contributors and 3 funder logos on /donate, two separate sections on two separate pages, both reached by plain text links in a footer “ABOUT” column. Its footer names funders in prose, not logos.

Also worth deciding separately: both Hyperliquid-native comparables (Hypurrscan, ASXN) monetise through a Hyperliquid referral CTA in the header - “Support Me”, “Trade · Save 4%” - not through sponsor logos. That is the ecosystem norm, it costs no visual real estate, and CHARTE.md:25-33 already writes the rules it would have to follow.
